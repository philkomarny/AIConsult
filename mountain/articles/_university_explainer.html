How Our AI Work Actually Works

What you'll build in this workshop, how it changes your morning, and why a person who starts every day knowing exactly what matters is the best thing that ever happened to the people they serve.

This workshop is not about AI tools. It's about you — your knowledge, your work, your judgment — and what becomes possible when you stop losing all of that to inbox noise and tool sprawl and the tyranny of whatever's loudest.

By the end of the day, you'll have two things. First: a personal knowledge vault on your own machine — your expertise, organized and owned by you. Second: a /today script that reads your vault every morning and tells you what actually matters. Not what's urgent. What matters. For the students you serve, for the work only you can do, for the things that would otherwise slip through the cracks.

That's the shift. A person who starts every day with real clarity about what their learners need — not a dashboard, not a report someone else built, but their own knowledge made usable — that person serves better. And when enough people at Maryville work that way, the learners feel it. The community feels it. Not because we built a system. Because we built a habit.

These five layers explain how that works — from what you build today, to what our learners experience because of it.

What you build today — and who it serves
Layer 1 What you build in the workshop
You leave with a vault — your knowledge, your machine, your rules
In the workshop, you'll build it. After today, you'll actually use it.

The first thing we do today is set up a personal knowledge base on your own computer. Not in the cloud. Not in a vendor's system. On your machine, in plain text files you own. We'll connect it to a private repository so it syncs securely — but the originals live with you.

This matters because most people's expertise lives nowhere. It's in their head, in their inbox, scattered across tools they don't control. The vault changes that. Your meeting notes, your research, the things you've learned about the students you work with — organized, linked, and accessible to an AI that you've pointed at your own context, not the internet's.

When the AI helps you think through something, it's drawing on what you know — not a generic model of what everyone knows. That's a fundamentally different kind of help. And it stays yours.

Why it matters for our learners A person who can actually find what they know — quickly, in context — makes better decisions for the students sitting across from them. The vault isn't a filing system. It's the foundation that makes everything else in this workshop possible.
→ enables
Layer 2 What your vault makes possible
You build a /today script — and your morning changes
The vault is the foundation. This is the payoff.

Once your vault exists, we build the thing that makes it matter every single day: a /today script. You run it in the morning. It reads your vault — your notes, your context, the things you've been tracking — and tells you what deserves your attention today. Not what's in your inbox. Not what someone else flagged as urgent. What actually matters, based on what you know.

Maybe it surfaces a student who's gone quiet and you'd been meaning to follow up. Maybe it reminds you that the curriculum revision you've been noodling on connects to something you wrote down three weeks ago. Maybe it just says: here are the three things that will make today count. The point is, it's built on your knowledge, not someone else's algorithm.

That's not productivity theater. That's a person who walks into every interaction a little more prepared, a little more present, a little more likely to catch the thing that would've slipped through. The people you serve feel the difference, even if they can't name it.

Why it matters for our learners A student doesn't know you ran /today that morning. They just know you remembered. You followed up. You saw the pattern. That kind of attention changes outcomes — and it compounds across every person a student interacts with.
→ enables
Layer 3 What happens when many people work this way
Empowered people start connecting the dots no one else can see
This isn't a system. It's what happens when the people closest to the work are finally equipped to act on what they know.

When one person runs /today, they serve their students better. When fifty people do it — advisors, faculty, program directors, financial aid — something else starts to happen. Not because anyone built a centralized brain. Because prepared people start having better conversations.

An advisor notices a pattern in the students they're tracking and mentions it to a program director who's been thinking the same thing from a different angle. A faculty member's curriculum notes connect to an employer need that someone in career services has been hearing about for months. These connections were always there. The difference is that people finally have the headspace and the context to make them.

None of this requires a platform or a mandate. It requires people who aren't drowning — people whose knowledge is organized, whose mornings start with clarity, and who have the bandwidth to notice what they'd normally miss.

Why it matters for our learners A student's experience improves when the people around them are coordinated — not by software, but by shared awareness. When your advisor, your professor, and your financial aid counselor are all a little more dialed in, you feel it. Even if nobody told them to talk to each other.
→ enables
Layer 4 What the learner actually feels
Someone finally sees them clearly — and responds like it
The learner doesn't know about your vault or your /today script. They just know something is different here.

A working adult with 15 years of healthcare experience and some prior college credits calls Maryville. In the old world, they get a brochure. A form. A "someone will get back to you." In this world, they get a person — an advisor who ran /today that morning, who has context, who can say: here's what counts, here's your path, here's what it costs, here's what to do next.

That's not a chatbot. That's not a platform. That's a human being who showed up prepared because their own tools made it easy. The learner doesn't need to know the infrastructure behind that conversation. They just need to feel like someone at this university actually sees who they are and what they need.

The same thing happens for the first-generation student who doesn't know the right questions to ask. For the employer trying to build a talent pipeline with a local partner. For the community member who assumes the university isn't for people like them. When the people doing the serving are equipped to see clearly, the people being served feel known.

Why it matters for our community Trust is built one interaction at a time. When a learner's first experience with Maryville feels personal, specific, and real — because the person they talked to was genuinely prepared — that's the kind of reputation no marketing budget can buy.

Layer 5 Why all of it matters
We reach the people the old system couldn't see
42 million Americans started college and never finished. They didn't fail — the system failed them.

These are working adults with real credentials, real skills, and real lives that made finishing impossible at the time. They're still out there. Most still want to finish. What they need isn't a better brochure — they need one person at a university who can see them clearly and say: here's your path.

That person is you. Not a system. Not a platform. You — with your vault organized, your /today script telling you who needs attention, your context sharp enough to have the conversation that changes someone's trajectory. The person who left school 15 years ago and finally calls back doesn't need an algorithm. They need a human being who's ready for that call.

Every layer below this one exists for that moment. The vault, the /today script, the clarity, the preparation — it all compounds so that when a learner who thought the door was closed finally knocks, someone answers who knows exactly how to open it. That's the mission. And it starts with what you build today.

Why it matters for our community The universities that earn the trust of stopped-out adult learners won't do it with better marketing. They'll do it because their people were ready — genuinely, specifically ready — for the conversation. This workshop is how that readiness gets built.

"We don't need a smarter system. We need equipped people. Give someone their own knowledge back — organized, on their machine, in their hands — and watch what they do with it."

Phil Komarny · Chief Future Officer, Maryville University
